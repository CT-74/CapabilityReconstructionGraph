TITLE: A Runtime Capability Graph for External Behavior Composition in C++
SUBTITLE: From Discovery to N-Dimensional Hypergraphs
================================================================================

--- THE HOOK (The "Killer Phrase") ---
In most engines, adding a feature means touching a central registry. In the 
Capability Reconstruction Graph (CRG) pattern, adding a feature is a purely 
additive operation: you add a file, and the topology of the system expands 
automatically. The "Manager" doesn't manage; it observes.

--- ABSTRACT ---
As systems scale, the "God Object" and the "Central Registry" become the 
ultimate bottlenecks for build times and architectural sanity. We often turn 
to ECS or Type Erasure to decouple data from logic, but we remain 
structurally coupled to the "Manager" that binds them.

This talk introduces the Capability Reconstruction Graph (CRG), an architectural 
pattern that eliminates centralized registries in favor of emergent runtime 
projections. By leveraging intrusive linked structures and variadic template 
matrices, CRG allows behaviors to be defined externally and discovered 
automatically across binary boundaries.

We will live-code (progression style) through 9 stages:
1. Decentralized Discovery: Building a zero-boilerplate plugin system with 
   simple intrusive topology.
2. Identity & Fusion: Using Type Erasure to bind stable identities to 
   external behavior definitions without structural storage.
3. The N-Dimensional Shift: Moving beyond 2D mapping (Type -> Behavior) to 
   hyper-contextual resolution where capabilities emerge from the 
   intersection of Time (Epochs), Space (Biomes), and Authority.

Attendees will leave with a new mental model for system composition: one where 
the capability graph isn't a data structure you maintain, but a projection 
you observe.

--- KEY TAKEAWAYS ---
* How to implement a zero-allocation, registry-free discovery layer.
* Why intrusive structures are the secret weapon for cross-DLL extensibility.
* Techniques for N-dimensional behavior resolution using modern C++17/20 templates.
* Performance strategies: SBO (Small Buffer Optimization) and static cache theory.

--- TARGET AUDIENCE ---
Intermediate to Advanced C++ Developers, Engine Architects, and anyone 
struggling with massive, tightly-coupled modular systems.
