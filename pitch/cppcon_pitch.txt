# CppCon 2026 Pitch: Reconstructing Capabilities: The N-Dimensional Hypergraph
**Speaker:** Cyril Tissier
**Duration:** 60 Minutes
**Target Audience:** Intermediate/Advanced C++ Software Architects, Robotics Engineers

### The Hook (00:00 - 05:00)
"The Matrix doesn't manage; it observes." 
Start with the pain every developer knows: the 3000-line `FacilityManager.cpp` filled with `dynamic_cast`. Show how adding one drone breaks the factory. Establish the goal: Zero heap, zero registries, infinite scalability.

### Act 1: The Zero-Heap Foundation (05:00 - 20:00)
- **Stage 2:** The Type-Erased Shell. It's beautiful, but it uses `std::unique_ptr`. In robotics, the heap is lava.
- **Stage 3:** The `HardwareHandle`. Live-code the Small Buffer Optimization (SBO) implementation. Show the `static_assert` that protects the stack. 

### Act 2: Emergent Topology & The Trap (20:00 - 35:00)
- **Stage 4 & 5:** Build the intrusive linked list. The graph emerges from static initialization.
- **Stage 7 (The False Peak):** The RAII Trap. Demonstrate why using C++ scopes to register/unregister behaviors at runtime is a thread-safety nightmare. We don't mutate reality; we must change our lens.

### Act 3: The N-Dimensional Matrix (35:00 - 50:00)
- **Stage 8:** Introduce the Temporal Axis (SystemState). The graph is immutable; the coordinate changes.
- **Stage 9:** The Grand Finale. Variadic fold expressions. We drop in `Args...` (Weather, Power, Security). The Matrix resolves the exact behavior intersection in O(1). 

### Q&A (50:00 - 60:00)
Buffer for audience questions regarding SBO cache-misses and template compilation times.
